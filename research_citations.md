# Research Citations for Platform Statistics

## Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) & Medication Safety

### Key Findings from Peer-Reviewed Research:

1. **55-86% Reduction in Medication Errors**
   - Source: Devine et al. (2010), "The impact of computerized provider order entry on medication error prevention"
   - Published in: Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association
   - Finding: "Early research demonstrated the benefits of CPOE/CDS systems in reducing medication errors by as much as 55–86%"
   - Citation: Devine EB, et al. J Am Med Inform Assoc. 2010;17(6):672-681

2. **50% Reduction in Serious Medication Errors**
   - Source: Bates et al. (1998), "Effect of computerized physician order entry and a team intervention on prevention of serious medication errors"
   - Published in: JAMA
   - Finding: "Physician computer order entry decreased the rate of nonintercepted serious medication errors by more than half"
   - Citation: Bates DW, et al. JAMA. 1998;280(15):1311-1316
   - Cited by: 3,005 papers

3. **Moderate Evidence for ADE Reduction**
   - Source: Syrowatka et al. (2024), "Computerized Clinical Decision Support To Prevent Medication Errors and Adverse Drug Events"
   - Published in: NCBI/AHRQ Evidence Report
   - Finding: "Overall, CDSSs reduce medication errors and adverse drug events, with moderate- and low-certainty evidence, respectively"
   - Citation: Syrowatka A, et al. AHRQ Comparative Effectiveness Reviews. 2024

4. **Drug Interaction Checker Accuracy**
   - Source: Kheshti et al. (2016), "A comparison of five common drug–drug interaction software programs"
   - Published in: Journal of Research in Pharmacy Practice
   - Finding: "Lexi-Interact has high sensitivity (87–100%) and specificity (80–90%)"
   - Citation: Kheshti R, et al. J Res Pharm Pract. 2016;5(4):257-263
   - Cited by: 276 papers

5. **Machine Learning CDSS Performance**
   - Source: Chen et al. (2024), "Ability of machine-learning based clinical decision support system"
   - Published in: Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine
   - Finding: "Machine learning based CDSS, MedGuard, has an ability to improve patients' safety by triggering clinically valid alerts"
   - Citation: Chen CY, et al. Comput Methods Programs Biomed. 2024;243:107905
   - Cited by: 18 papers
