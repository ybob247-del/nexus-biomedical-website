# Discovery Call Cheat Sheet

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 minutes before call)

- [ ] Review their website/LinkedIn
- [ ] Check which product they inquired about
- [ ] Have pricing sheet ready
- [ ] Open Google Sheet to take notes
- [ ] Have Calendly link ready for follow-up

---

## Call Structure (30-45 minutes)

### 1. Introduction (5 min)
**Your script:**
> "Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time to chat! I'm [Your Name] from Nexus Biomedical. I saw you reached out about [Product Name]. Before I dive into how we can help, I'd love to learn more about your situation. Does that work for you?"

**Goal:** Build rapport, set agenda

---

### 2. Discovery Questions (20-30 min)

## SECTION A: Company/Organization Background

### Q1: Tell me about your organization
- **What to listen for:**
  - Size (# of providers, patients, employees)
  - Type (hospital, clinic, pharma, device company)
  - Geographic reach (single location vs. multi-site)

**Follow-up:**
- "How many [providers/patients/submissions] do you handle per [month/year]?"
- "Do you have multiple locations?"

---

### Q2: What's your current process for [the problem]?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Manual vs. automated
  - Pain points
  - Time spent
  - Error rates
  - Cost

**Examples by product:**
- **RxGuardâ„¢:** "How do you currently check for drug interactions?"
- **ReguReadyâ„¢:** "How do you navigate FDA regulatory pathways today?"
- **ClinicalIQâ„¢:** "How do you currently design and optimize clinical trials?"
- **ElderWatchâ„¢:** "How do you monitor resident health and prevent falls?"
- **PediCalcâ„¢:** "How do you calculate pediatric medication doses?"
- **SkinScanâ„¢:** "How do you currently screen for skin cancer?"

---

### Q3: What's not working with your current approach?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Specific pain points
  - Frequency of issues
  - Impact (cost, time, safety)

**Follow-up:**
- "How often does this happen?"
- "What's the impact when it goes wrong?"
- "What have you tried to fix it?"

---

## SECTION B: Technical Requirements

### Q4: What systems do you currently use?
- **What to listen for:**
  - EHR system (Epic, Cerner, Allscripts)
  - Other software integrations needed
  - Data sources
  - Security requirements

**Follow-up:**
- "Do you need integration with [EHR name]?"
- "Do you have any compliance requirements?" (HIPAA, SOC 2, etc.)
- "Do you need SSO/SAML for user authentication?"

---

### Q5: How many users would need access?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Number of users
  - User types (doctors, nurses, admins)
  - Growth plans

**This determines pricing tier!**

---

## SECTION C: Decision Process

### Q6: Who else is involved in this decision?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Decision makers (CIO, CMO, CFO, CEO)
  - Influencers (department heads, IT)
  - Procurement process

**Follow-up:**
- "What's your typical process for evaluating new software?"
- "Do you need to go through procurement?"
- "What's your budget approval process?"

---

### Q7: What's your timeline?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Urgency (days, weeks, months)
  - Deadline drivers (regulatory, budget cycle, patient safety)
  - Start date

**Follow-up:**
- "What's driving this timeline?"
- "When do you need to have this in place?"

---

### Q8: What's your budget range?
**How to ask (don't be shy!):**
> "To make sure I propose the right solution, what budget range are you working with? Our solutions range from [low] to [high] depending on features and scale."

- **What to listen for:**
  - Budget allocated
  - Flexibility
  - Approval process

**If they won't share budget:**
> "No problem! Let me share our typical pricing tiers and you can tell me what makes sense..."

---

## SECTION D: Success Criteria

### Q9: What would success look like 6 months from now?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Specific metrics
  - Outcomes they care about
  - How they'll measure ROI

**Examples:**
- "Reduce medication errors by 50%"
- "Cut FDA submission time from 18 months to 12 months"
- "Prevent 80% of falls in our facility"

---

### Q10: What would make you choose us vs. staying with your current process?
- **What to listen for:**
  - Deal-breakers
  - Must-have features
  - Concerns

---

## 3. Solution Presentation (10 min)

**Now that you understand their needs, present YOUR solution:**

> "Based on what you've shared, here's how [Product Name] can help..."

**Focus on THEIR pain points:**
- "You mentioned [pain point] - our platform solves this by [feature]"
- "Since you need [requirement], we offer [capability]"
- "For your [size/type] organization, I'd recommend [tier]"

**Show relevant demo:**
- "Let me show you a quick example of how this works..."
- Screen share the interactive demo
- Walk through a scenario similar to their use case

---

## 4. Pricing Discussion (5 min)

**Be transparent about pricing:**

> "For your needs - [# users], [key features] - I'd recommend our [Tier Name] plan. That's [price] per [month/year]."

**If they need custom features:**
> "For the custom integrations you mentioned, we'd need to create a custom enterprise package. Based on similar customers, that typically ranges from [low] to [high]. I can put together a detailed proposal for you."

## 5. Next Steps (5 min)

**Always end with clear next steps:**

### If they're ready to move forward:
- [ ] Send proposal within 24 hours
- [ ] Schedule follow-up call to review proposal
- [ ] Introduce to technical team (if needed)

### If they need time:
- [ ] Ask what information they need
- [ ] Set specific follow-up date
- [ ] Send summary email with key points

**Your script:**
> "Great! Here's what I'll do: I'll send you a detailed proposal by [date] with pricing and implementation timeline. Then let's schedule a follow-up call for [date] to review and answer any questions. Does that work?"

---

## Post-Call Actions (Immediately after call)

- [ ] Update Google Sheet with all details
- [ ] Send follow-up email within 2 hours
- [ ] Create proposal (if qualified)
- [ ] Schedule follow-up call
- [ ] Set reminder for follow-up date
