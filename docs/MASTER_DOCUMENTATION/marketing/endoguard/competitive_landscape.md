## Detailed Analysis - Eli Health

**URL:** https://eli.health/

**Business Model:** Hardware + Software (Saliva-based hormone testing device + mobile app)

**Key Features:**
- Real-time, at-home hormone testing from saliva
- Patent-pending technology (over a dozen patents)
- Tests available: Cortisol, Progesterone, Estradiol (waitlist), Testosterone (waitlist)
- Results in 20 minutes via mobile app scanning
- Continuous monitoring capability (multiple tests over time)
- Personalized insights and hormone scores

**Scientific Approach:**
- Clinical advisors include board-certified endocrinologists, OB-GYNs, and reproductive specialists
- 5+ years of R&D at Canadian headquarters
- Focus on precision medicine and biomarkers
- Evidence-based content library on hormone impacts

**Target Audience:**
- Consumers interested in biohacking and precision health
- Women tracking fertility, perimenopause, menopause
- Athletes and performance-focused individuals
- People managing stress and metabolic health

**Unique Value Proposition:**
- "Continuous hormone monitoring" - not just point-in-time testing
- Instant results at home (vs. lab visits or mail-in tests)
- Affordable and convenient (compared to traditional lab testing)
- Tracks hormone fluctuations throughout the day/week/month

**Awards/Recognition:**
- CES 2025 Best of Innovation Winner (Digital Health category)
- Featured in major media outlets

**Strengths:**
- Hardware innovation creates barrier to entry
- Real-time data collection enables pattern recognition
- Strong clinical advisory board
- Focus on continuous monitoring vs. one-time testing

**Potential Gaps:**
- Limited to hormones that can be measured in saliva
- Requires hardware purchase (upfront cost)
- Focus more on tracking than on clinical decision support or root cause analysis
- Limited emphasis on environmental factors (EDCs, microplastics)
- More consumer wellness-focused than clinical intelligence

## Clinical Decision Support Systems (CDSS) - Reference Category

### Key Players in Clinical Decision Support

1. **UpToDate (Wolters Kluwer)**
   - URL: https://www.wolterskluwer.com/en/solutions/uptodate
   - Evidence-based clinical solutions for healthcare providers
   - Trusted reference for clinical, drug, and patient content
   - Industry standard for point-of-care clinical information

2. **Micromedex (Merative)**
   - URL: https://www.merative.com/newsroom/micromedex-launches-ai-powered-search
   - AI-powered search for drug information and toxicology (launched Sept 2025)
   - Clinical decision support for medication management
   - Focus on drug-drug interactions and safety

3. **BMJ Clinical Intelligence**
   - URL: https://clinicalintelligence.bmj.com/
   - Computable guideline recommendations for healthcare providers
   - Evidence-based clinical pathways
   - Integration with EHR systems

4. **Clint Health**
   - URL: https://clinthealth.com/platform/
   - Patented platform for evidence-based patient-therapy matching
   - Point-of-care decision support based on clinical state
   - Focus on personalized treatment recommendations

5. **Optum Clinical Decision Support**
   - URL: https://business.optum.com/en/operations-technology/clinical-decision-support.html
   - Combines clinical and financial data
   - Focus on appropriate, high-value care
   - Enterprise healthcare solutions

6. **AmalgamRx**
   - URL: https://amalgamrx.com/cds-marketplace/
   - 1000+ pre-built clinical guidelines
   - EHR integration capabilities
   - Continuous guideline updates

### Key Characteristics of CDSS:
- Evidence-based knowledge consolidation
- Point-of-care integration (EHR systems)
- Drug-drug interaction checking
- Medication safety alerts
- Clinical guideline implementation
- Real-time decision support for clinicians
- Focus on improving patient outcomes and reducing errors

### Relevance to Hormone Intelligence Platform:
The "RxGuard for hormones" concept suggests building a CDSS specifically for:
- Hormone-related conditions and symptoms
- Environmental exposures (EDCs, microplastics)
- Complex multi-system hormone interactions
- Evidence-based treatment pathways
- Clinician education and support
- Patient empowerment with clinical-grade information
