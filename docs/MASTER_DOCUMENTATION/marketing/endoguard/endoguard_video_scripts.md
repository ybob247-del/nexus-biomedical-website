### Target Audience
General public, educational content for awareness building

### Distribution Channels

### Tone
Educational, accessible, empowering

### SCRIPT

**[VISUAL: Animated title card]**

**TEXT:** "What Are Endocrine Disruptors? | EndoGuard™ Education Series"

**[VISUAL: Host appears—friendly science communicator]**

**HOST:**
You've probably heard the term "endocrine disruptors" or "EDCs." But what are they, really? And why should you care?

**[VISUAL: Animated endocrine system diagram]**

**HOST (VOICEOVER):**
Let's start with the basics. Your endocrine system is a network of glands that produce hormones—chemical messengers that regulate virtually everything in your body.

**[VISUAL: Hormones labeled: estrogen, testosterone, thyroid, cortisol, insulin]**

Energy. Mood. Weight. Sleep. Reproduction. Metabolism. All controlled by hormones.

**[VISUAL: Cut back to host]**

**HOST:**
Now, endocrine disruptors are chemicals that interfere with this system.

**[VISUAL: Animated chemical structure approaching hormone receptor]**

**HOST (VOICEOVER):**
They can mimic your natural hormones, tricking your body into responding when it shouldn't.

**[VISUAL: Chemical blocking receptor]**

They can block hormones from binding to their receptors, preventing your body from responding when it should.

**[VISUAL: Chemical affecting hormone production]**

Or they can interfere with how hormones are made, stored, or broken down.

**[VISUAL: Cut to host]**

**HOST:**
The result? Hormone chaos.

**[VISUAL: Montage of everyday products]**

**HOST (VOICEOVER):**
And here's the scary part: endocrine disruptors are everywhere.

**[VISUAL: Products labeled with EDC names]**

BPA in plastic bottles and food containers. Phthalates in cosmetics and fragrances. Triclosan in antibacterial soap. PFAS in non-stick cookware. Parabens in lotions. Pesticides on produce.

**[VISUAL: Research citations appearing]**

**HOST (VOICEOVER):**
