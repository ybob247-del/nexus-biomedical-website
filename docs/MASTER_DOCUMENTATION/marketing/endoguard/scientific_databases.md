# Key Scientific Databases and Sources for Hormone Intelligence Platform

## Primary Medical Literature Databases

### 1. PubMed / MEDLINE
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
**Provider:** National Library of Medicine (NLM), National Institutes of Health (NIH)
**Coverage:** 39+ million citations for biomedical literature
**Content:** MEDLINE, life science journals, online books
**Access:** Free
**Key Features:**
- Most comprehensive biomedical database
- Indexed with Medical Subject Headings (MeSH)
- Advanced search capabilities
- API access available for programmatic queries
**Relevance:** Essential for hormone research, endocrinology, environmental health

### 2. PubMed Central (PMC)
**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
**Provider:** NIH National Library of Medicine
**Coverage:** Free full-text archive of biomedical literature
**Content:** Full-text journal articles (not just abstracts)
**Access:** Free, open access
**Key Features:**
- Full-text searching
- Includes figures, tables, supplementary materials
- API access for bulk data retrieval
**Relevance:** Access to complete research papers for detailed analysis

### 3. Cochrane Library
**URL:** https://www.cochranelibrary.com/
**Provider:** Cochrane Collaboration
**Coverage:** High-quality, independent evidence for healthcare decisions
**Content:** Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, clinical trials
**Access:** Some free content, subscription for full access
**Key Features:**
- Gold standard for evidence-based medicine
- Systematic reviews of interventions
- Rigorous methodology
**Relevance:** Evidence-based treatment recommendations for hormone conditions

### 4. EMBASE
**Provider:** Elsevier
**Coverage:** Biomedical and pharmacological database
**Content:** 32+ million records, emphasis on drug research
**Access:** Subscription required
**Key Features:**
- Strong coverage of European and international journals
- Excellent for drug interactions and pharmacology
- Complementary to PubMed
**Relevance:** Drug-hormone interactions, pharmacological treatments

### 5. ClinicalTrials.gov
**URL:** https://clinicaltrials.gov/
**Provider:** U.S. National Library of Medicine
**Coverage:** Database of clinical research studies worldwide
**Content:** Study protocols, results, recruitment status
**Access:** Free
**Key Features:**
- Current and completed trials
- Results database
- API access available
**Relevance:** Latest hormone therapy trials, treatment outcomes

## Critical Research Areas: Environmental Hormone Disruptors

### Key Research Findings on Microplastics & EDCs

#### Microplastics and Endocrine Disruption
- **Major Review (2023)**: "A review of the endocrine disrupting effects of micro and nano plastic" (Cited 267 times)
  - PMC: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9885170/
  - Findings: Long-term exposure exhausts thyroid function, affects reproductive hormones
  - Impact: Microplastics themselves (not just additives) can alter sex hormones

- **Rutgers Study (2023)**: Plastic particles themselves alter hormones, independent of chemical additives
  - URL: https://www.rutgers.edu/news/plastic-particles-themselves-not-just-chemical-additives-can-alter-sex-hormones

#### Endocrine Society Position
- **Official Statement (2020)**: "Plastics pose threat to human health"
  - URL: https://www.endocrine.org/news-and-advocacy/news-room/2020/plastics-pose-threat-to-human-health
  - Key Point: Plastics contain and leach EDCs that threaten human health
  - Authoritative source for clinical guidance

#### Specific Chemical Concerns

**Bisphenol A (BPA)**
- Acts as xenoestrogen (mimics estrogen)
- Interferes with natural hormone binding
- Linked to: reproductive disorders, metabolic dysfunction, obesity
- Reduces testosterone and estrogen levels
- Blocks thyroid hormone action
- Reference: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6864600/ (Cited 176 times)

**Phthalates**
- Known endocrine disruptors
- Affect estrogen and testosterone
- Impact child development and reproductive health
- Linked to: infertility, metabolic disorders, behavioral issues
- EPA/NIEHS research: https://www.epa.gov/research-grants/niehsepa-cehcs-novel-methods-assess-effects-bisphenol-phthalates-child-development

**Environmental Estrogens (Xenoestrogens)**
- Found in: plastics, pesticides, industrial chemicals, personal care products
- Health impacts: feminization, reproductive disorders, cancer risk
- Linked to: breast cancer (women), prostate cancer (men)
- Major review (2017): Cited 1,237 times - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28040262/

#### Health Impacts Documented in Literature

**Reproductive Health**
- Infertility in both men and women
- PCOS and ovarian dysfunction
- Reduced sperm quality
- Disrupted menstrual cycles
- Early puberty

**Metabolic Disorders**
- Obesity and weight gain
- Insulin resistance and diabetes
- Thyroid dysfunction
- Metabolic syndrome

**Hormonal Cancers**
- Breast cancer
- Prostate cancer
- Endometrial cancer
- Thyroid cancer

**Neurobehavioral Effects**
- ADHD and learning disabilities
- Anxiety and depression
- Cognitive impairment
- Altered parental and social behaviors

**Other Systems**
- Cardiovascular disease
- Immune system dysfunction
- Bone health impacts
- Developmental disorders in children
